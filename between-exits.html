Issue Zero · Photo Essay

Between
Exits

Ikebukuro and Shinjuku are places of arrival, departure, and constant negotiation.

Photography and words by Eastokyo · Tokyo, 2026

Ikebukuro and Shinjuku are often described through scale: the crowds, the stations, the signs, the number of people passing through. But movement has quieter details—the pause beneath a signal, the descent into a tunnel, the moment before a ticket gate opens.

These photographs follow routes between street level and underground Tokyo, where every arrival is already becoming another departure.

01 · Street level

The city as interface.

Directions compete for attention. Station exits divide the neighborhood into mental maps. People read signals, storefronts, traffic, and one another without stopping. The street works because thousands of small decisions happen at once.

02 · Below street level

Light, footsteps, anonymity.

Underground, distance is measured differently. A turn, a staircase, a bank of signs. The crowd compresses, then separates. Faces become silhouettes for a moment before everyone emerges somewhere else.

03 · Through the gates

Every journey continues.

Ticket gates are both boundary and rhythm. A tap, a tone, a step forward. The city receives one journey and immediately releases the next.
