About Eastokyo

Attention

A photography-led magazine about the people, places, routines, and overlooked details that make Tokyo feel lived in.

Not a guidebook. Not a postcard.

Eastokyo is an independent editorial project made in Tokyo. It begins with photographs, then stays long enough to notice what those photographs contain: movement, work, waiting, repetition, construction, weather, light, and the small negotiations of ordinary life.

The name does not describe a fixed border on a map. It describes a point of view—looking outward from the center, toward neighborhoods and routes that are often passed through more quickly than they are seen.

How the magazine looks.

Specific places. Original photography. Clear writing. No tourist shorthand and no invented reporting.

01

Stay local

Stories begin with real streets, stations, shops, rooms, and people—not a generic idea of Japan.

02

Look slowly

The ordinary becomes editorial material when attention is patient enough: a crossing, a clock, a temporary barrier, a route upward.

03

Show the work

Photography leads, but captions, sequencing, and context turn a group of images into a magazine story.

Issue Zero: Tokyo in motion.

The opening issue follows movement through Ikebukuro and Shinjuku, then pauses for four shorter observations from the city: a staircase, a construction character, a public clock, and infrastructure under revision.

Issue Zero is both a first edition and a statement of intent. Eastokyo will continue building outward through photo essays, field notes, neighborhood stories, portraits, and visual records of everyday change.
