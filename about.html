About Eastokyo

ETK-BUREAU // FILE 00-ABOUT

Tokyo's Most Questionable Newsroom

Eastokyo is a satirical English-language publication covering Tokyo, Japan, bureaucracy, culture, technology, civic weirdness, and the slow emotional collapse of anyone who has ever filled out a municipal form.

Bureau East Tokyo
Status Unofficial
Approval Stamped Anyway

Mission

We report the news Japan deserves, fears, denies, and politely reschedules.

Our job is to cover the emotionally accurate version of Tokyo: the city of immaculate train platforms, mysterious paperwork, mascots with too much influence, and public notices that somehow know your deepest insecurities.

承認

Editorial Policy

If it feels true, sounds official, and involves at least one fax machine, we investigate immediately and then pretend a committee told us to stop.

確認

Fact Checking

Every story is reviewed by editors, interns, vending machines, and one temple cat with suspicious access to municipal filing cabinets.

Internal Procedure

How an Eastokyo story becomes officially suspicious

Step 01

Observe

A citizen sighs near a ticket gate. A vending machine flickers. A committee is born.

Step 02

Overanalyze

We compare the incident against train posters, local customs, seasonal anxiety, and mascot behavior.

Step 03

Stamp

The article receives a fake document number, an unnecessary seal, and one dramatic headline.

Manifesto

Satire is journalism wearing festival clothes.

Eastokyo exists because modern life in Japan is beautiful, absurd, hyper-organized, emotionally restrained, deeply strange, and occasionally controlled by mascots with better branding than most elected officials.

We are not here to mock Tokyo. We are here to report the version of Tokyo that appears after the third train transfer, the second apology, and the fourth form nobody told you to bring.
